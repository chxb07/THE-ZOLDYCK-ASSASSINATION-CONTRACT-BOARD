ZOLDYCK CONTRACT OPTIMIZATION STRATEGY

1. Contract Selection Algorithm
--------------------------------
Contracts are evaluated using a scoring function:

score = (gold * risk_factor) / (travel_time + execution_time)

Where:
- travel_time is derived from the map distances
- execution_time is the contract duration
- risk_factor penalizes traps and complications

Adjustments:
- Trap contracts are heavily penalized and often abandoned
- Contracts with tight deadlines are deprioritized unless nearby
- Contracts that unlock new skills are slightly boosted

Only contracts that satisfy current skill requirements are considered.

At each decision step, contracts are sorted by score and the best candidate is selected.

--------------------------------

2. Route Optimization Approach
--------------------------------
Travel is modeled using a distance matrix (map.json).

Strategy:
- Prefer contracts in the same or nearby cities
- Avoid long-distance jumps unless reward is significantly higher
- Minimize idle travel days

A greedy approach is used:
- Always move to the highest scoring reachable contract
- Travel time is explicitly simulated day-by-day

--------------------------------

3. Skill Progression Model
--------------------------------
Each completed contract increases the required skills used in that contract.

This creates a progression system:
- Early game: prioritize easy contracts to build skills
- Mid game: mix skill farming and medium reward contracts
- Late game: focus on high-value contracts unlocked by skills

Skill growth directly expands the pool of available contracts.

--------------------------------

4. Handling Uncertainty (Complications & Traps)
--------------------------------
Complications:
- Modeled as a 20% probability of increasing execution time by 1.5x
- The scoring function accounts for this by reducing expected value

Traps:
- Identified after acceptance
- Immediately abandoned if detected
- Each abandonment increases reputation penalty

Reputation penalty:
- Each failure or abandonment reduces future rewards by 10% (stacking)
- Strategy avoids excessive penalties by being selective

--------------------------------

5. Tradeoffs Considered
--------------------------------
Gold vs Time:
- High gold contracts are not always optimal if travel time is large

Risk vs Reward:
- Risky contracts are penalized unless reward is exceptional

Skill Farming vs Immediate Profit:
- Early investment in skills yields higher long-term gains

Greedy vs Long-Term Planning:
- Greedy approach is used but indirectly supports long-term growth via skills

--------------------------------

6. Overall Strategy Summary
--------------------------------
- Start with low-risk contracts to build skills
- Gradually transition to higher-value contracts
- Avoid traps and excessive travel
- Maintain steady progression without incurring high penalties

The system balances efficiency, growth, and risk management to maximize total gold within 200 days.
